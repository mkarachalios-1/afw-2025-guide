2. OBJECTIVE

The Pilot’s Guide provides all participants with the necessary information and instructions for arrivals, departures, rehearsal, and the main event.

3. EXECUTION

All participating aircraft and formations should arrive on 3rd or 4th September. The departure plan is made per crew requests; main departures are on 8 September. Late flight plan submission may result in ATC rejecting the plan.

3.1. Arrival of participating aircraft

Arrivals must plan for at least 20 minutes of holding fuel. Arrivals outside slot may expect delays or be redirected. Crews must stay near their aircraft to supervise turnaround, then report to the Flying Control Committee.

3.2. Departure of participating aircraft

Departures are planned for Monday, 8 September; slot allocation is first-come, first-served. Only in exceptional cases or due to weather may departures be approved for Tuesday, 9 September.

4.2. Display Regulations
  • All flying under Hellenic National Regulations.
  • Display profiles must be approved by the host nation/FDD.
  • Maneuvering:
    • Towards crowd: no closer than 450 m.
    • Away from crowd: no closer than 250 m (runway edge).
    • Max speed: 0.9 Mach or 600 KIAS (whichever is lower).
    • Min display heights: Fixed-wing: 500 ft AGL (inside DATZ, outside airfield); 1,000 ft AGL over Skhimatari; Helicopters: 100 ft AGL (demo), 200 ft AGL (maneuver).
    • Parachute displays: main canopy open by 2,500 ft AGL; exceptions for FAI certificate holders.
    • Civil ex-military jets: no aerobatics below 500 ft unless specifically authorized.
    • No overflight of spectators unless authorized in writing.
    • Twilight/pyrotechnic displays: special rules, minimum 2,000 ft AGL, pyros vectored away from crowd.
    • RC Models: may use south taxiway; always respect minimum separation.
4.3. Display Restrictions
  • No engine shutdown during displays.
  • No intentional asymmetric flight.
  • No interlocking maneuvers toward spectators.
  • Stay within aircraft limitations; avoid overflight of spectator areas unless approved.
  • Respect minimum heights when crossing airfield boundaries; climb before crossing.
  • Smoke off before landing/touch & go.
  • Dispense of flares by aircraft during displays will be allowed by the FDD based on the daily Fire Risk level published by the Hellenic Ministry for Climate Crisis and Civil Protection (www.civilprotection.gov.gr/en/xartis).
  • Only essential crew allowed during flying displays.
4.4. Flying Control Committee

FCC members are identified at the briefing and monitor all rehearsals and displays. If display limits are exceeded, the FDD will broadcast STOP – STOP – STOP; pilot must immediately terminate display and land.

4.5. Display Safety Briefing / Debriefing
  • Mandatory safety briefings at Tanagra AFB, briefing room near Apron C.
  • Friday, 5 Sep: Rehearsal briefing 09:00.
  • Saturday & Sunday, 6-7 Sep: Display briefing 09:00.
  • Pilots/team leaders must debrief with FDD by phone within 30 min of landing.
4.6. Documentation
  • Required for civilian participants: Pilot’s License, Certificate of Test or Experience, Medical Certificate, Display Authorization, Aircraft Registration, Airworthiness, Insurance.
  • ICAS Individual Risk Management Form must be completed/submitted before rehearsal or display.
4.7. Flying Display Program and Planning
  • Detailed display program at daily briefings.
  • Strict adherence to published timings; changes by FDD or radio.
  • Changes to display sequence/timing: inform FDD immediately.
  • Unapproved/no rehearsal: participation may be restricted or cancelled.
  • Display start: brakes release/takeoff or entering DATZ. Display end: vacating runway or exiting DATZ.

4.9. Holding

Four holding areas (EAST, WEST, NORTH, SOUTH) inside Tanagra TMA. Standard right-hand orbit at assigned altitude, always inside DATZ. Primary arrival hold: EAST Hold. See Annex D1 for reference points.

4.10. Phraseology
  • Terminate Call: Stop display for external reasons (e.g. birds); display may resume if safe.
  • STOP Call: Used for pilot safety/competence; display must NOT resume.

4.12. Emergencies
  • In-flight: Immediately stop display, inform ATC, follow instructions for landing/diversion.
  • Diversion Airfields: Elefsis AFB (LGEL), Dhekelia (LGTT), Nea Anchialos (LGBL). See Annexes.
  • Bail-out areas: Two near Tanagra AFB, see Annex E.
  • Runway Cables/Barriers: Present at each end of 10C/28C.
  • Hydrazine/Hot Brakes: Park in prescribed areas; await fire brigade. See Annex F.
  • Comms Failure: On ground: shut down/advise marshal; In holding: follow-me vehicle; Airborne: fly north side of display line at 500 ft AGL, rock wings, comply with visual tower signals.
4.13. Ramp Operations, Engineering Services, and Refueling
  • Ground Support Team will marshal/tow aircraft as needed. Crews to provide suitable tow bar if possible.
  • Servicing is the responsibility of Captain/Team Leader; disposal of waste POL strictly controlled.
4.14. Accommodation
  • Accommodation arranged by organizers, depending on AFW agreement.
  • Mess Hall: meals at workplace; dinner buffet at VIP Tent on Sunday, 15 September.
  • Crew area: Snack bar at Officers' Mess; refreshments and toilets during display days.
4.15. Transportation
  • Dedicated transport to/from hotels at scheduled hours; otherwise self-arranged.
  • Shuttle buses at Tanagra AFB between operational areas.
  • Military Police may conduct breathalyzer tests (limit 0.2 mg/ml).

    ANNEX H. TERMINOLOGY
    • Aerobatic Maneuver: As defined in Air Navigation Order.
    • Aircraft Parking Area: Used for aircraft parking, no public access during display.
    • Airfield Boundary: Delineated by fence or operational use.
    • ATC: All approved ground-to-air radio transmissions.
    • Crowd Line: Closest edge of any spectator-accessible area to the display area.
    • Display Area: Airspace within which display aircraft are maneuvered.
    • Display Authorization (DA): Document outlining aircraft types/groups a pilot may display.
    • Display Item: Single aircraft or formation flying as one display act.
    • Display Line/Axis: Track and distance along which displaying aircraft may operate.
    • Display Routine: Linked maneuvers to be performed during a flying display.
    • Display Pilot: Pilot with DA/exemption for display flight.
    • Essential Personnel: Authorized for access to restricted areas during display.
    • Flying Control Committee (FCC): Safety management group for the display.
    • Minimum Aerobatic Height: Most restrictive of pilot's DA, FDD, or national rule.
    • More definitions and explanations provided in full annex.
    ANNEX I. ICAS Individual Risk Management/Assessment Worksheet

    All pilots must complete and submit the ICAS Individual Risk Management/Assessment Worksheet prior to any rehearsal or flying display. The worksheet includes:

    • Pilot/Team/Organization name, type of display, aircraft type, date.
    • Risk assessment questions on pilot currency, aircraft status, weather, and display authorization.
    • Signatures required. See annex for the full worksheet format.
